The Must Sees of Cozumel!

The San Gervasio Ruins

The San Gervasio Ruins are another must see in Cozumel. A Mayan complex thought to have been the site of the sanctuary of Ixchel, goddess of fertility. This was in important pilgrimage site for Mayan women to worship at. You will definitely need a jeep to for this jaunt, as these hidden ruins are not accessible without 4x4.

Chichen Itza

Despite the 3-hour drive north of Playa del Carmen, Chichen Itza is not to be missed. These Mayan ruins attract millions of visitors every year and are one of the wonders of the world. But be sure to bring cash (pesos not dollars), as it is not uncommon for their credit card machines to not be working.

"El Cielo"

"El Cielo" Cozumel, known as "Heaven," is one of the best sites in Cozumel and should definitely be on your list of places to see. Only accessible by a 25-minute boat ride from the nearest cruise pier and about a mile from shore, it's shallow crystal clear water as far as the eye can see. With the turquoise hue famous to Cozumel, it is covered with soft white sand that massages your feet as you walk. A great place to hunt for starfish and see stingrays up close (they are harmless).

Arricife Palancar

What would a trip to Cozumel be without a visit to our famous reefs for prime scuba diving and snorkeling? We attract diving and snorkeling enthusiasts from around the world. The Arricife Palancar (Palancar Reef) is several kilometers long and one of the best with 230ft of visibility and stunning coral formations. The north end of the reef is shallower and home to brightly colored sea creatures to see on your dive or snorkel adventure.
